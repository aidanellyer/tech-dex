CPU
Information

The CPU, is often referred to as the computer’s brain. The CPU controls the tasks that run on your computer and how fast those tasks are completed. While there are a lot of specs you can use to compare CPU’s, for your first build it’s OK to find one that’s a little cheaper but gets the job done. Ask friends who also have computers what processors they have and how they like them.



PSU
Information

The PSU or “Power Supply Unit” outputs power towards the, motherboard, hard drives and other storage devices, LEDs and much more. Power supplies are powered through a 3 pin kettle cable that draws power from a power outlet. You can get different types of power supplies. Like Bronze power supplies, silver, gold and platinum. These come in different types of version, like 80 PLUS Gold.


Case
Information

A computer case, also known as a computer chassis, computer tower or system unit or cabinet, is the enclosure that contains most of the computers components excluding the display keyboard and mouse. The cases are usually made from steel or aluminium. Some pc cases have a side window that is made with tempered glass. Computer cases can be a small box, the small cases are used to build compact PC’s, but then you can also get a large pc case this is the one that is mainly referred to as a tower.



GPU
Information

A GPU or “Graphics Processing Unit” is an expansion card that goes into a PCI slot. For older less powerful graphics cards this does not require an extra power cable however, more recent and more powerful graphics card can require 4/8/12 pin connectors. These Graphics Cards often use 1-6 fans depending on there performance. These cards often output “images” or pixels to a display such as a monitor or tv. Graphics Cards can combine their power through “SLI” which will use both the graphics cards power.
